# Security Policies Overview

**Ready-to-use security policy templates for UK trades businesses**

---

## üéØ Purpose of These Policies

Security policies document **how your business handles security**. They:
- Set clear expectations for staff
- Demonstrate compliance to customers
- Provide evidence for certifications (Cyber Essentials, GDPR)
- Protect your business legally
- Guide decision-making when security issues arise

**These aren't just paperwork - they're your security playbook.**

---

## üìö Policies Included

### 1. [Password Policy](password-policy.md)
**What it covers:** Password creation, storage, management, and multi-factor authentication

**Why you need it:**
- Cyber Essentials requires password controls
- Weak passwords are the #1 attack vector
- Staff need clear guidance on password requirements
- Password manager deployment needs documentation

**Key requirements:**
- 12+ character passwords
- Password manager mandatory
- MFA on critical systems
- No password sharing
- Unique passwords per system

**Implementation time:** 2 hours to customize, 1 week to deploy

---

### 2. [Mobile Device Policy](06-mobile-device-policy.md)
**What it covers:** Security for company phones, tablets, and BYOD (personal devices used for work)

**Why you need it:**
- Engineers have customer data on phones
- Cyber Essentials requires mobile device management
- GDPR requires securing personal data
- Lost phones are a major risk

**Key requirements:**
- Screen locks (PIN/fingerprint) mandatory
- Find My Device enabled for remote wipe
- Updates applied within 30 days
- Work data separated from personal
- Lost device reporting procedure

**Implementation time:** 2 hours to customize, 1 day to deploy to devices

---

### 3. [Data Protection Policy](data-protection-policy.md)
**What it covers:** How you handle customer and employee personal data (GDPR compliance)

**Why you need it:**
- UK GDPR legal requirement
- Customers increasingly ask for this
- Needed for tenders and contracts
- Functions as your privacy policy for website

**Key requirements:**
- What data you collect and why
- Legal basis for processing
- How long you keep data
- Who you share it with
- Data subject rights procedures
- Breach notification process

**Implementation time:** 3 hours to customize, can double as website privacy policy

---

### 4. [Incident Response Policy](incident-response-policy.md)
**What it covers:** What to do when security incidents occur (lost phones, phishing, breaches, etc.)

**Why you need it:**
- Panic causes bad decisions
- GDPR requires documented breach response
- Fast response limits damage
- Everyone needs to know who to contact

**Key requirements:**
- Clear reporting procedure (who to call, when)
- Step-by-step response for common incidents
- Incident severity levels
- Evidence preservation
- Notification timelines (ICO, customers)
- Post-incident review process

**Implementation time:** 2 hours to customize, 30 minutes to brief team

---

### 5. [Acceptable Use Policy](acceptable-use-policy.md)
**What it covers:** Rules for using company IT systems, equipment, and resources

**Why you need it:**
- Sets expectations for appropriate use
- Protects company legally
- Prevents security risks from misuse
- Balances personal use with security

**Key requirements:**
- Acceptable vs. prohibited activities
- Personal use boundaries (reasonable use OK)
- Internet and email guidelines
- Software installation rules
- Remote access and BYOD rules
- Monitoring and enforcement

**Implementation time:** 2 hours to customize, 15-minute team briefing

## üéØ Which Policies Do You Actually Need?

### Minimum for Basic Compliance
**Must have:**
- Password Policy (authentication basics)
- Data Protection Policy (GDPR requirement)

**Should have:**
- Mobile Device Policy (if staff have phones with customer data)
- Incident Response Policy (know what to do when things go wrong)

**Nice to have:**
- Acceptable Use Policy (sets expectations)

### For Cyber Essentials Certification
**Required:**
- Password Policy (access control requirement)
- Mobile Device Policy (mobile device management requirement)
- Acceptable Use Policy (secure configuration requirement)

**Strongly recommended:**
- Data Protection Policy (data security requirement)
- Incident Response Policy (shows you're prepared)

### For Full GDPR Compliance
**Required:**
- Data Protection Policy (core GDPR documentation)
- Incident Response Policy (breach notification procedures)

**Recommended:**
- Password Policy (security measures)
- Mobile Device Policy (data security on mobile)
- Acceptable Use Policy (appropriate data handling)

### Our Recommendation: All Five
**Why implement all five:**
- Takes only 2-4 weeks total
- They work together as a system
- Complete coverage for CE + GDPR
- Professional appearance to customers
- Peace of mind

**They're all templates - customization is quick.**

## üìù How to Customize Policies

### Step 1: Read the Plain English Section
Understand what the policy does and why before customizing.

### Step 2: Download/Copy the Template
Get the formal policy section into a document you can edit.

### Step 3: Replace Placeholders
Find and replace all instances of:
- `[COMPANY NAME]` ‚Üí Your actual company name
- `[DIRECTOR NAME]` ‚Üí Actual name
- `[EMAIL]` / `[PHONE]` ‚Üí Actual contact details
- `[DATE]` ‚Üí Today's date
- Any other [BRACKETED] items

### Step 4: Adjust for Your Business
Modify sections that don't quite fit:
- Software you use (Xero, Tradify, etc.)
- Number of employees
- Specific devices (Samsung phones, etc.)
- Your actual procedures

**Keep 80% of the template, customize 20%.**

### Step 5: Have Someone Else Read It
Get office manager/colleague to review:
- Is it clear and understandable?
- Does it match what you actually do?
- Any confusing sections?

### Step 6: Save and Version
- Save in company folder (OneDrive, etc.)
- Add version number (v1.0)
- Add review date (annually)
- Keep both Word and PDF versions

## üéì Training Staff on Policies

**Don't just email policies and expect staff to read them.**

### Effective Training Approach

**1. Team Meeting (30 minutes)**
- Explain WHY you're implementing policies
- Cover key points from each policy
- Use plain English, not policy language
- Answer questions
- Make it interactive

**2. Hands-On Demonstrations**
- Show password manager in action
- Demonstrate MFA setup
- Walk through reporting an incident
- Practice with real examples

**3. Written Materials**
- Provide policy documents
- Create one-page quick reference guide
- Emergency contact cards
- FAQ sheet

**4. Follow-Up**
- Check understanding in first week
- Answer questions as they arise
- Provide additional help where needed

**5. Annual Refresher**
- Review policies annually
- Update with any changes
- Reinforce key points

## ‚ùì Common Questions

### "Do we really need formal written policies?"

**Yes, for several reasons:**
- Cyber Essentials certification requires them
- GDPR compliance requires documented procedures
- Customers/tenders increasingly ask for evidence
- Staff need clear expectations
- Protects you legally if something goes wrong

**Without policies:** You're winging it, no evidence of compliance, vulnerable legally.

---

### "Can't we just have one 'Security Policy' instead of five?"

**You could, but:**
- One huge policy is overwhelming to read
- Different policies serve different purposes
- Staff only need relevant policies (engineers don't need full GDPR policy)
- Easier to update individual policies as things change
- Cyber Essentials expects separate policies for different controls

**Separate policies are clearer and more usable.**

---

### "How often do we update policies?"

**Minimum: Annually**

**Also update when:**
- Regulations change (new GDPR guidance, etc.)
- Technology changes (new systems, new software)
- Business changes (new locations, significantly more staff)
- After security incidents (learn and improve)

**Set annual review date when you create each policy.**

---

### "Do policies need to be signed by staff?"

**Best practice: Yes**

**Why:**
- Confirms they've read and understood
- Evidence for compliance (CE, GDPR)
- Protects company if policy violated
- Creates accountability

**How:**
- Simple acknowledgment form
- Digital signature or written
- Keep in employee files
- Update when policies change

---

### "What if staff don't follow the policies?"

**That's what Incident Response and Acceptable Use policies cover:**
- Minor violations: Verbal reminder, retraining
- Moderate violations: Written warning
- Serious violations: Formal disciplinary action
- Gross misconduct: Potential dismissal

**But most violations are accidental - focus on training and support, not punishment.**

---

### "Can we show these to customers/auditors?"

**Data Protection Policy: Yes, publish it**
- Goes on your website as privacy policy
- Share with customers who ask about GDPR
- Include in tenders/proposals

**Other policies: Share summaries**
- Don't share full internal policies externally
- Create summary documents for customers
- Explain your security measures without revealing details
- "Yes, we have password policies requiring MFA and password managers"
