About This Tool
What this is, how it works, and why it's built this way.

What this tool is

A web-based security assessment and action planning tool for small UK trades businesses, built around the case study of NorthWest Heating & Gas Ltd — a Greater Manchester plumbing and heating company. It generates prioritised recommendations, a printable checklist, and a staff briefing email based on a short assessment of the user's business.

Part of a wider UK Trades Cybersecurity Kit aligned to NCSC Cyber Essentials, NCSC Small Business Guidance, and UK GDPR (ICO). This web version exists because the intended audience — small business owners and office managers — are not running Python scripts. A tool only works if it reaches the person who needs it.

Why plain English throughout

James at NorthWest Heating tested an early draft of their mobile policy on Dave, one of the engineers. Dave said he'd "probably skim it and sign it." That was the signal to rewrite everything from scratch.

The final policy uses phrases like "use common sense" and language James would actually use in a team meeting. It is not legally weaker for this — it is more likely to be read, understood, and followed. A policy nobody reads is not a control. Readability is a security property.

How recommendations are prioritised

Recommendations are generated from five scored inputs: MFA status, screen lock status, password manager status, email system, and data handling practices. The "Do This Week" tier maps directly to the controls whose absence most frequently precedes real incidents in small business breach data: no MFA, no screen locks, reused passwords.

Priority logic reflects NorthWest Heating's actual implementation order — they fixed MFA and phone locks in week one because those were the fastest wins with the highest impact. The checklist follows the same sequence.

Key decisions NorthWest Heating made

Company phones not BYOD for engineers: Phones get dropped and lost on job sites. Company-owned means Sarah can lock or wipe without complication. 6 × Samsung Galaxy A34 refurbished cost ~£900 total.

Technical setup before the announcement: Sarah set up Find My Device and Bitwarden on all phones before James said anything to the team. When he announced the policy at the Friday meeting, he could say "it's already done — here's your Bitwarden login." This removed the fear of hassle. People comply with things that are already easy.

Fingerprint not PIN as primary lock: Engineers unlock phones constantly on job sites. A complex password would be abandoned in practice. Fingerprint is fast; 6-digit PIN as backup. Auto-lock at 5 minutes (one exception documented for an engineer who needed 10 minutes for reading job sheets on site).

Bitwarden free tier, not paid: Sarah evaluated it against 1Password. Bitwarden free covers everything they need. LastPass was explicitly ruled out after their 2022 breach. The open-source audit trail matters for a security tool.

Assumptions and limitations

Calibrated for small UK trades and field-service businesses (2–25 employees). Recommendations are based on NCSC Cyber Essentials and ICO guidance. Businesses in regulated sectors (financial services, healthcare, legal) will have additional obligations this tool does not address. NorthWest Heating is a realistic composite case study; all names are fictional.

Governance note

This tool is a portfolio thinking exercise demonstrating GRC tooling for non-technical audiences. The design decisions — plain English, real cost data, worked examples throughout, printable outputs, staff email generator — reflect how security guidance actually reaches the people who need to act on it. The most important question about any security tool is not whether it is comprehensive. It is whether the person who needs to use it will open it.
