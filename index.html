Common Questions

The questions that actually come up when trades businesses start thinking about security. Honest answers, no jargon. Read the full FAQ →

"Do I actually need to worry about this? I'm a small heating company, not a bank."
Yes, but proportionately. You're not a target for sophisticated hackers. You are a realistic target for automated phishing, credential attacks, and ransomware — which increasingly hits small businesses because they have weaker defences. The more practical reason: larger commercial customers and public sector contracts now ask about security as part of tender processes. And you hold customer home addresses and access details. GDPR applies to you.
"I've been in business 15 years and never had a problem. Why change now?"
You probably have had problems you didn't know about. The average time between a breach occurring and being discovered is over 200 days. The threat landscape has changed — ransomware that used to target large enterprises now routinely hits small businesses. And customers ask now. If you can't show you take security seriously, you lose quotes.
"One of my lads refuses to put a PIN on his phone. What do I do?"
Most resistance to screen locks is about the inconvenience of typing a PIN. Fingerprint solves it — it's quicker than typing anything. If the objection is something else, find out what it actually is. The direct version: it's a company phone. You get to set the requirements for company phones.
